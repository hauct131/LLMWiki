## Definition

Low resolution observations refer to imagery captured with limited detail and clarity due to factors such as small sensor size, short exposure times, or significant atmospherical interference. These images have fewer pixels per inch (PPI) compared to high-resolution counterparts.

## Why It Matters

Low resolution observations are crucial for studying celestial objects that cannot be resolved by ground telescopes due to their vast distances from Earth, providing valuable data despite the lack of detail.

## Variants / Related Terms

Superfine imaging synthesis (SFIS), Aperture masking interferometry

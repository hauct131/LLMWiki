## Definition

In super-resolution (SR), an aperture refers to the opening through which light passes in optical systems. By manipulating this opening's size and shape during image capture or reconstruction, one can influence the resolution of the final high-quality output images from low-resolution inputs.

## Why It Matters

The control over an aperture is crucial for optimizing light collection efficiency and minimizing diffraction limits in SR techniques to enhance detail extraction beyond conventional imaging capabilities.

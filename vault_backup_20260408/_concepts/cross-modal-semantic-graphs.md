## Definition

Cross-Modal Semantic Graphs are graphical representations used in multimodal data analysis, linking elements from different sensory modalities (e.g., text and images) through shared semantic concepts to understand their interconnections better. These graphs facilitate the integration of heterogeneous information sources by mapping them onto a common conceptual space.

## Why It Matters

Cross-Modal Semantic Graphs enable more effective sentiment analysis in multimodal contexts, enhancing machine understanding beyond single modality data interpretation.

## Variants / Related Terms

Multimodal Integration Networks (MIN), Heterogeneous Information Fusion Models (HIFM)

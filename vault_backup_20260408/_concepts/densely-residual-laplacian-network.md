## Definition

The Densely Residual Laplacian Network (DRLN) is an advanced neural network design that incorporates residual learning with the structured connectivity of a graph, aiming to enhance feature representation. It achieves this by using densely connected layers and integrating localized spectral filters based on the graph's Laplacian matrix.

## Why It Matters

DRLN offers improved performance in tasks requiring complex pattern recognition due to its efficient learning of hierarchical representations with fewer parameters than traditional deep networks.

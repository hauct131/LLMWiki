## Definition

Iterative Denoising Processes refer to a series of steps where noise is incrementally removed from an image or signal while preserving its essential features. This technique often involves multiple passes over data and relies on mathematical models that describe the statistical properties of noise, allowing for progressive enhancement with each iteration.

## Why It Matters

Iterative Denoising Processes are crucial in improving image quality by reducing artifacts without losing detail or introducing blur during tasks like super-resolution and restoration.

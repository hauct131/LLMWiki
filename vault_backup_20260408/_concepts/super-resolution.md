## Definition

Super-resolution refers to techniques used in image processing that enhance the resolution of an imaging system, producing higher quality and more detailed pictures from lower-quality sources without acquiring new data. It often involves sophisticated algorithms such as interpolation or machine learning models like convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

## Why It Matters

Super-resolution is crucial for improving the visual fidelity of images in various applications, including medical imaging and satellite image analysis.

## Variants / Related Terms

Interpolation methods, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN)

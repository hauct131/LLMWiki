## Definition

Multi-modal Named Entity Recognition (MNER) is an advanced form of named entity recognition that incorporates both textual and visual data to identify entities within content across different modes or media types such as social media posts. It leverages the combined information from images, often accompanying texts like captions or hashtags, alongside natural language processing techniques for more accurate detection and categorization of entities in multimedia contexts.

## Why It Matters

Because it enhances entity recognition systems' accuracy by utilizing visual cues that are frequently present with textual content on social media platforms where both types often coexist to provide richer information about the subject matter.

## Definition

A Recursive Convolutional Network is a deep learning architecture that applies recursive operations to process data through multiple convolutional layers iteratively or hierarchically, enabling the extraction of complex features from input signals such as images and audio. This structure often involves self-similarity at different scales within the same layering system.

## Why It Matters

Recursive Convolutional Networks are significant for their ability to capture multi-scale patterns in data, which is crucial for tasks like image recognition where contextual information can vary greatly across resolutions.

## Variants / Related Terms

Deeply-Recursive Convolutional Network (DRCN), Hierarchical CNN

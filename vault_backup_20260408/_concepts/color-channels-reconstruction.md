## Definition

Color Channels Reconstruction in digital imaging refers to the process of accurately restoring or enhancing each color channel (Red, Green, and Blue) within an image that may have been degraded due to factors like compression artifacts, sensor noise, or transmission errors. This technique ensures that all three channels are processed uniformly for a consistent visual quality in high-resolution outputs.

## Why It Matters

Color channel reconstruction is crucial for maintaining the fidelity and natural appearance of images when enhancing resolutions through super-resolution techniques, especially important in fields like medical imaging or satellite photography where detail accuracy can be critical.

## Definition

The CapsNet model is a type of neural network architecture that incorporates "capsules" - groups of neurons with shared weights and dynamic routing mechanisms to process data hierarchically. It was designed by Hinton et al. as an alternative approach for tasks like image recognition, where it excels in preserving spatial relationships between features.

## Why It Matters

CapsNet models have significantly improved the performance of neural networks on visual perception and classification problems due to their ability to maintain detailed feature representations throughout layers.

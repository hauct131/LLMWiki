## Definition

Pre-trained models are machine learning algorithms that have been previously trained on large datasets and can be fine-dicted or further trained for specific tasks such as aspect-based sentiment analysis (ABSA). These models leverage learned features from vast amounts of data, which helps in achieving higher accuracy with less task-specific training.

## Why It Matters

Pre-trained models significantly reduce the time and resources required to develop effective ABSA systems by providing a solid starting point for further fine-tuning on domain-specific datasets.

## Variants / Related Terms

BERT, GPT (Generative Pretrained Transformer), RoBERTa

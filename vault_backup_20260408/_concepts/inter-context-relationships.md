## Definition

Inter-context relationships refer to the connections or interactions between different contexts within text, where multiple discourse elements influence each other'thy understanding of specific topics or entities mentioned in a given passage. These relationships are crucial for comprehending nuanced meanings and sentiments that emerge from complex texts.

## Why It Matters

Understanding inter-context relationships is essential for accurate sentiment analysis, as it allows the identification of how different aspects within various contexts affect each other's perceived attitudes or emotions in a text.

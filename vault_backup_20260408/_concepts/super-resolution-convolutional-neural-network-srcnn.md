## Definition

The Super-Resolution Convolutional Neural Network (SRCNN) is a deep learning algorithm specifically designed for image super-resolution. It employs three convolutional layers, each with its own set of learned filters to reconstruct high-frequency details from low-resolution images and enhance their quality by upscaling them in resolution while preserving sharpness and reducing artifacts.

## Why It Matters

SRCNN significantly improved the performance of super-resolution techniques, offering a more effective alternative to traditional interpolation methods for enhancing image clarity without lossy compression effects.

## Variants / Related Terms

BM3D (Bilateral Filtering with 3x3 Convolution), EDSR (Enhanced Deep Super-Resolution)

## Definition

Weighted Averaging High-Resolution Output refers to a technique in deep learning for image super-resolution where multiple high-resolution predictions are combined using weighting schemes that emphasize certain outputs over others, based on their estimated quality or relevance. This approach is designed to enhance the final reconstructed image's fidelity beyond what single prediction models can achieve alone.

## Why It Matters

It improves upon traditional super-resolution methods by producing clearer and more accurate high-definition images from low-res inputs, which are crucial for applications requiring detailed visual information such as medical imaging or satellite image analysis.

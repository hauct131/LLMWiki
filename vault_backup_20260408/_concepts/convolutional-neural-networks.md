## Definition

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are a class of deep neural networks commonly used in analyzing visual imagery but also applicable to other data types. They employ layers with convolving filters that automatically and adaptively learn spatial hierarchies of features from input images, which makes them particularly effective for tasks involving image recognition or processing sequences where local patterns (like edges in an image) are important indicators of higher-level constructs such as objects within the scene.

## Why It Matters

CNNs have revolutionized computer vision by providing a robust mechanism to automatically learn features without manual extraction, leading to significant advancency in tasks like object detection and facial recognition.

## Variants / Related Terms

AlexNet, VGG (Very Deep), Residual Networks (ResNets)

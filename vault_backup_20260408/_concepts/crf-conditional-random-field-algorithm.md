## Definition

A Conditional Random Field (CRF) algorithm is a probabilistic graphical modeling tool used for sequence prediction tasks where context matters. It models the conditional probability distribution at test time given observed variables and incorporates both past and future information in its predictions, making it suitable for structured output learning such as labeling sequences or segmentation of data into regions with similar characteristics.

## Why It Matters

CRFs are significant because they provide a framework to effectively capture the dependencies between observations over time, leading to more accurate and contextually relevant sequence predictions in various applications like natural language processing (NLP) and bioinformatics.

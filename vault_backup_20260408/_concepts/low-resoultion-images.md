## Definition

Low resolution images are visual representations with limited pixel density and detail clarity. These images have fewer pixels per unit area compared to high-resolution counterparts, resulting in less sharpness and potential loss of information during zooming or printing at larger sizes.

## Why It Matters

Understanding low resolution is crucial for developing techniques like super-resolution that enhance image quality by artificially increasing the number of pixels to improve detail perception.

## Variants / Related Terms

Underexposed images, Compressed images (JPEG), Thumbnail versions

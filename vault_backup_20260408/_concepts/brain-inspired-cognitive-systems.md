## Definition

Brain Inspired Cognitive Systems refer to computational models and systems designed based on the principles of brain functioning, aiming to replicate human cognition processes such as learning, memory, perception, and decision-making. These systems often employ neural networks or other biologically inspired algorithms for processing information in a manner analogous to how neurons operate within the central nervous system.

## Why It Matters

They hold potential for advancing artificial intelligence by creating more efficient and adaptive computing solutions that can learn from experience, similar to human learning processes.

## Variants / Related Terms

Spiking Neural Networks (SNN), Artificial Neurodynamics Systems

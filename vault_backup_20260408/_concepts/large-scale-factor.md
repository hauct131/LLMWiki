## Definition

The Large Scale Factor refers to the extent of an area over which image processing or analysis must be conducted. It indicates how much larger than the object's actual size a region is being examined, often in context with satellite imagery where objects may span several kilometers on Earth’ extraterrestrial bodies like Mars and Venus as well.

## Why It Matters

Understanding large scale factors is crucial for accurate interpretation of images across different environments or resolutions by compensating for the loss of detail when zooming out from a small to larger area context in image processing tasks such as remote sensing, astronomy, and medical imaging.

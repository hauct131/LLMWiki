## Definition

Continuous Recurrent Factorization Machines (CRFs) are probabilistic graphical models that combine the representational power of factorization machines with recurrent neural networks to capture sequential patterns and contextual information. They process input data in a way that accounts for both local features within individual elements, as well as broader dependencies across sequences or time steps.

## Why It Matters

CRF models are significant because they provide an effective framework for sequence modeling tasks such as natural language processing and temporal pattern recognition where context plays a crucial role in understanding the data structure.

## Variants / Related Terms

Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, Gated Recurrent Units (GRU)

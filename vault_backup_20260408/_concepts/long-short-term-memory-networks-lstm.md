## Definition

Long Short-Term Memory Networks (LSTM) are a special kind of Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) capable of learning long-term dependencies. They achieve this through mechanisms known as gates that regulate the flow and retention of information within the network, mitigating issues like vanishing gradients which commonly plague standard RNNs when processing sequences with longer intervals between relevant events or features.

## Why It Matters

LSTM networks are crucial for tasks involving sequential data where context over long periods is essential to understanding and prediction accuracy, such as natural language processing (NLP) applications including aspect-level sentiment classification in reviews.

## Variants / Related Terms

Gated Recurrent Unit Networks (GRU), Peephole LSTM networks

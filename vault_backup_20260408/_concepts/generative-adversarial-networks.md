## Definition

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) are a class of artificial intelligence algorithms used in unsupervised machine learning that involve two neural networks, namely the generator and discriminator, competing against each other to generate new data samples with similar characteristics as the training set. The generator creates synthetic images or sequences from random noise input, while the discriminator evaluates them alongside real-world examples, guiding the generator's improvement iteratively until it becomes adept at producing convincing outputs.

## Why It Matters

GANs have revolutionized fields such as computer vision by enabling high-quality image generation and transformation tasks like super-resolution without explicit paired training data requirements.

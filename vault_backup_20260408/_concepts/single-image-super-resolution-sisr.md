## Definition

Single Image Super-Resolution (SISR) refers to the process of reconstructing a high-resolution image from a single low-resolution source. It involves sophisticated algorithms that infer finer details not present in the original, lower resolution input.

## Why It Matters

SISR enhances visual quality and detail for better analysis or improved user experience without needing additional high-quality images.

## Variants / Related Terms

Deep learning methods (e.g., Convolutional Neural Networks), traditional interpolation techniques, Generative Adversarial Networks

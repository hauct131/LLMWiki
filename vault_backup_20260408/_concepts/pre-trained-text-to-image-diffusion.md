## Definition

Pre-trained Text-to-Image Diffusion refers to a method where text prompts are used as input for generative models that have been previously trained on large datasets to produce corresponding images. This process involves gradually transforming noise into an image through iteratively refining the generation, guided by the provided text description.

## Why It Matters

It enables high-quality synthesis of detailed and contextually relevant visual content from simple text descriptions with applications in various fields such as design, entertainment, and medical imaging.

## Variants / Related Terms

Progressive Diffusion GANs (PDGAN), Denoising Score Matching Networks (DSMN)
